Hello and welcome to The Cricket League !


The one stop shop for all the fans wanting a little more than the random horde of tours and games going on; and having no consequence with a ranking format that too complex to comprehend. There are currently 10 test playing nations, and surely they play each other at home and away in a period of 10 years; but who knows for sure. The system, and the scheduling has been way too complicated, for way too long, for anyone to actually take interest; and by anyone, I mean tournament-cricket-loving fans like me. With the same 10 playing nations play against each other with nothing to lose except pride - it is getting a bit boring, don't you say. So, here's my two cents on a system that would bring value, significance, and most of all CONTEXT to cricket.

Rules and Format


The league consists of the Top 9 teams in the ICC Test Championship. Each team plays each other twice - once at home, and once way. Only the first game of the tour would be considered i.e the first Test of the tour. The league starts after the end of the 2015 WORLD CUP, that ended on the 29th March, 2015. The reason for the exclusion of teams other than the Top 9 are because the tours with the minor nations happen rarely.
